About Us

Crime against women is any crime committed against a woman or girl because of her gender. It can include physical violence, sexual violence, emotional abuse, economic abuse, and stalking. Crime against women is a serious problem that affects millions of women and girls around the world. There are many different laws in place to protect women from crime. These laws vary from country to country, but they typically include laws against domestic violence, sexual assault, and harassment. In some countries, there are also laws specifically against female genital mutilation and forced marriage. If you are a victim of crime against women, it is important to know your rights and to seek help. You can contact your local police department or a victim's advocacy organization for assistance. Here are some examples of laws that protect women from crime: Domestic violence laws: These laws make it illegal to abuse or threaten a family member or household member. Sexual assault laws: These laws make it illegal to force someone to have sex against their will. Harassment laws: These laws make it illegal to harass someone with unwanted or offensive behavior. Female genital mutilation laws: These laws make it illegal to perform female genital mutilation on a girl or woman. Forced marriage laws: These laws make it illegal to force someone to get married against their will. If you are a victim of crime against women, you have the right to: Be safe and free from violence. Be treated with respect and dignity. Have access to support services. File a report with the police. Go to court and have your case heard. If you are a victim of crime against women, please know that you are not alone. There are people who can help you. Please reach out to a local police department or victim's advocacy organization for assistance.
